# Monthly Performance Report — Nike Netherlands
### February 2026 | Compared to January 2026

---

## Executive Summary

February was a **strong month** for Nike NL. Total spend of **€58,420** across 4 platforms generated **€198,640 in tracked revenue**, delivering a **blended return of €3.40 for every €1 spent** — up 9% from January. Google Ads was the highest-returning channel at 4.6x, Facebook maintained solid performance at 3.2x despite a mid-month creative fatigue issue that was resolved within 5 days. The biggest untapped opportunity remains mobile conversion: fixing the checkout friction identified in our analysis could unlock an estimated €15,000-€20,000 in additional monthly revenue from existing traffic.

### Platform Assessments

**Google Ads** — Best-performing channel for the third consecutive month. Search campaigns delivered strong Quality Scores (avg 7.8) and Shopping captured high-intent buyers efficiently. Performance Max is steady but opaque — we evaluate it purely on output metrics. Impression share for Shopping is at 74%, meaning there's room to scale.

**Facebook & Instagram** — Solid overall, though we navigated a creative fatigue episode mid-month on prospecting campaigns (frequency hit 4.2, cost per customer spiked 40%). We rotated in new testimonial-based creatives on Feb 14 and performance recovered within 5 days. Retargeting remains exceptionally strong at 5.1x return.

**TikTok Ads** — The standout growth story this month. Return improved 18% after introducing UGC-style content that resonated strongly with the 18-34 demographic. Hook rates averaged 34% on the new creative batch (vs 22% on the old). Still a smaller channel but proving its value.

**LinkedIn Ads** — Generating leads at €78.72, well below the €100 B2B benchmark. Lead quality is solid — 34% of February leads have progressed to opportunity stage in HubSpot (vs 22% industry average).

## What Worked This Month

1. **Google Shopping — feed optimization paid off.** New product titles and descriptions improved Quality Scores across the board (avg 6.2 → 7.8). Click-through rates increased 15%, and cost per customer dropped 12%. This single change was the largest contributor to the month's performance improvement.

2. **Fast creative fatigue response on Facebook.** When prospecting creatives fatigued mid-month (frequency 4.2, CPA +40%), we detected it within 48 hours and rotated in new testimonial-based ads. Performance recovered to baseline within 5 days, preventing an estimated €2,800 in wasted spend.

3. **TikTok UGC strategy validated.** The shift to UGC-style content (unboxing, "get ready with me" format) drove an 18% improvement in return on spend. Hook rates averaged 34% on new content vs 22% on older polished creative. This confirms TikTok audiences respond to authenticity over production value.

4. **LinkedIn lead quality improving.** February leads converted to opportunity at 34% (up from 22% in January). The shift to industry-specific content (benchmark reports) is attracting more qualified decision-makers.

5. **Anomaly system caught a spend spike early.** On February 12, the Google PMax campaign began overspending at 2.4x the daily average. The automated alert caught it within 3 hours, and the budget was corrected — preventing an estimated €1,200 in unplanned spend.

---

## What Needs Improvement

1. **Mobile conversion rate gap.** Mobile converts at 1.4% vs desktop at 3.8% — a 63% gap. Mobile drives 64% of sessions but only 38% of revenue. The primary friction point is the shipping cost reveal at checkout (68% mobile abandonment vs 41% desktop). **Fixing this is the single largest revenue opportunity — estimated €15,000-€20,000/month in recoverable revenue.**

2. **Facebook prospecting creative lifespan too short.** Prospecting creatives averaged only 16 days before fatigue onset (vs 28 days for retargeting). This creates production pressure. Need to build a larger creative library (target: 8-10 active variants instead of current 3-4) to extend time between full refreshes.

3. **Organic Search underperforming.** SEO traffic converts at 1.0% — half the 2% e-commerce benchmark. Landing pages for top organic keywords don't match search intent well. The top 5 organic landing pages have a 38% engagement rate (below the 55% benchmark), suggesting content-to-intent mismatch.

4. **TikTok creative lifespan is very short.** Despite strong performance, TikTok creatives average only 12 days before fatigue. This requires 2-3 new videos per month to maintain performance. Recommend establishing a monthly content production cadence specifically for TikTok.

5. **Attribution gap widening on Facebook.** Facebook reported 840 conversions; GA4 attributed 612 to Paid Social. The 27% gap is larger than the typical 15-20%. Possible causes: increased view-through conversions or cross-device journeys. Worth monitoring but not actionable — GA4 remains the source of truth for budget decisions.

## How We Measure Success

*Google Analytics 4 (last-click session attribution) is the single source of truth for all revenue and customer numbers in this report. Each advertising platform counts conversions differently — Facebook includes view-through conversions (people who saw but didn't click an ad), Google Ads uses data-driven attribution (distributes credit across touchpoints). These methods will always produce higher numbers than Google Analytics, which only credits the last click. This discrepancy is normal, expected, and doesn't indicate a tracking error. We use platform numbers for optimizing within each channel, and Google Analytics for cross-channel budget decisions and total revenue reporting.*
