## What Worked This Week

### 1. Google Shopping delivered record-low cost per customer
The Shopping campaign achieved a cost per customer of just **€8.50** — the lowest in 3 months. This was driven by improved product feed titles we implemented two weeks ago, which boosted click-through rates by 15%. The Quality Score on top product categories improved from 6 to 8.

> **Recommendation:** This campaign has room to grow — we're only showing in 72% of eligible searches. Increasing the daily budget by 15% should capture more of these high-intent shoppers.

### 2. Facebook retargeting returned €5.20 for every €1 spent
The "Spring Collection — Retargeting" campaign continued its strong performance with a **5.2x return** (up from 4.1x last week). Website visitors who saw our retargeting ads converted at 4.8%, well above the 2.3% site average. This audience remains responsive to the current creative rotation.

> **Recommendation:** Scale this campaign by 20% — the audience is still converting efficiently and we haven't hit frequency saturation yet (current frequency: 2.1).

### 3. New TikTok creative outperformed account benchmarks
The "Spring Unboxing" video achieved a **42% hook rate** (percentage of viewers who watched at least 3 seconds), significantly above the 26% account average. This UGC-style creative is resonating with the 18–34 demographic and has driven 18 conversions at €24 cost per customer — below our €35 target.

---

## What Needs Attention

### 1. Facebook prospecting costs are climbing
The "Prospecting — Lookalike" campaign saw cost per customer increase by **35%** this week (from €28 to €38), and frequency has reached 3.8. This is a classic sign that the audience has seen these ads too often. The current creative has been running for 23 days.

> **Root cause:** Creative fatigue — the same audience is seeing the same ads repeatedly, leading to declining engagement.
> **Proposed action:** We've prepared two new creative concepts (testimonial video + UGC carousel) to rotate in. Recommend pausing the current top-spending ad and introducing the replacements.

### 2. LinkedIn "Decision Makers" campaign generated zero leads
This campaign spent **€340** this week with no conversions. The click-through rate (0.28%) is below the 0.6% benchmark for Sponsored Content, suggesting the ad copy or offer isn't resonating with this audience.

> **Root cause:** Weak offer-to-audience fit. The whitepaper topic may be too broad for the C-suite targeting.
> **Proposed action:** Test a more specific offer (e.g., industry benchmark report) with updated copy. If no improvement in 7 days, recommend pausing and reallocating budget to Google.

### 3. Mobile conversion rate remains significantly below desktop
Google Analytics shows mobile visitors convert at **1.4%** versus **3.8% on desktop** — a 63% gap. Mobile accounts for 64% of all sessions but only 38% of revenue. This is a website experience issue, not an advertising issue.

> **Root cause:** Mobile checkout flow — we've identified the shipping cost reveal at checkout as the primary drop-off point (68% abandonment on mobile vs 41% on desktop).
> **Proposed action:** This is a website optimization priority. Recommend showing estimated shipping earlier in the mobile experience and testing a simplified mobile checkout.

## How We Measure Success

*A note on measurement: We use Google Analytics 4 as the single source of truth for revenue and customer numbers. Each advertising platform (Facebook, Google, LinkedIn) counts conversions using its own method — Facebook includes people who saw an ad but didn't click (view-through conversions), while Google distributes credit across multiple touchpoints. These different counting methods mean the platforms will always show higher numbers than Google Analytics. This is normal and expected. The "blended return" figures in this report use Google Analytics revenue, which provides the most conservative and reliable baseline.*
